The Khmer Empire was a powerful state in Southeast Asia, lasting from 802 CE to 1431 CE.
Its capital was Angkor, one of the largest pre-industrial cities in the world.
Angkor Wat, built in the early 12th century, is a symbol of Khmer architecture and religion.
Jayavarman VII was one of the greatest kings, expanding the empire and building hospitals and temples.
The fall of Angkor occurred due to invasions from the Siamese and internal instability.
